About Alpe d'Huez

Alpe d'Huez is one of France's most iconic ski resorts, often referred to as "The Island in the Sun" thanks to its 300 days of sunshine per year. The resort is part of the Grand Domaine Ski area and offers more than 250 kilometers of slopes, ranging from wide beginner-friendly runs to challenging black pistes.

Among its highlights is the legendary Sarenne, the world's longest black run at 16 kilometers – a true bucket-list experience for skiers. Situated at 1,860 meters with slopes reaching up to 3,330 meters at the summit of Pic Blanc, the ski area benefits from reliable snow conditions.

Après-Ski & Activities

Bars & Nightlife

The town has a lively atmosphere with plenty of après-ski opportunities. Popular spots include Smithy's Tavern, Underground Bar, and La Folie Douce – perfect for enjoying drinks with fellow students.

Dining

In the evening, the resort offers a wide variety of restaurants serving both French specialties and international cuisine.

Off-Slope Activities

Beyond skiing, enjoy ice skating, swimming, wellness centers, winter hiking, or even a husky sled ride!

Practical Info

Transport

Bus transport to and from the destination is included in the price. Departures from both Lyngby and Ballerup Campus. Remember your passport!

Luggage: One large soft bag (not hardcase!), one small bag for inside the bus, a bag with boots and a bag with ski/snowboard.

You can choose to fly for an extra fee or organize transport yourself for a price reduction.

Après-Ski

At La Folie Douce, enjoy cold drinks with fellow students and the guide team. Special offers for those wearing a Summit Week bracelet!
